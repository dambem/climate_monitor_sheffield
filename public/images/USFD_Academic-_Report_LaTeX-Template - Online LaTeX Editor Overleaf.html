4.5 Further Work
The work done in this paper provides a starting point for the creation of realistic animal
gaits, but it additionally provides a platform through which other research may be done. As
currently there does not exist an open source method of simulating and measuring animal
gaits on quadrupeds, this research could provide a potential starting point for the develop-
ment of realistic animal movements. Due to the accuracy of pybullet when converting from
simulation to real robotics, as seen in , further research could potentially be done on the
results seen in this work to an actual quadruped robot. As the models seen in this work have
been directly based on the laikago robot, an investigation could be made as to results seen in
an actual robot. If a full system identification is made, this could increase the accuracy of the
results seen in the paper. Additionally, it would allow us to work out the cost of locomotion
through the power expended by the robot, and help produce a more detailed explanation.
This paper has only dealt with gaits in a limited environment, as the only investigation
made was confining the animal to travel down a straight line. With the implementation of
limb feedback, additional work could be done in effects on the animals CPG through the
introduction of obstacles in the environment. More work could potentially be done on the
introduction of control into the system, allowing the gaits to change dynamically based on the
velocity chosen, as well as a possible implementation of turning through changing coupling.
Although out of scope for the project due to time constraints, further work can be done
on implementing evolutionary techniques for this project, and investigating as to whether
they would reach the same minimas as seen in this paper.
Although some work was done on investigating gaits apart from walking, additional work
should be done investigating the effectiveness of faster gaits at larger values. One of the
main issues with using faster gaits for this project was due to stability, as the robot did not
contain any methods of remaining stable when using faster gaits, causing it to fall down when
attempting to go quickly. By the implementation of feedback for walking, as well as potential
balancing methods as seen in , faster gaits, as well as transitions between gaits could be
investigated.
Additional work could potentially be done on the application of dynamic similarity to
different quadrupeds, through the extension of work done on different models, as the central
pattern generator designed in this paper could easily be extended to different robotic models.
Chapter 5
Conclusions
This paper has established and created a method of creating and investigating animal gaits
through the replication of central pattern generators. Although there exists some issues with
the implementation of these gaits, this paper produces a framework by which additional
work on quadruped movements can be performed. Additionally, we have found that the
walking gait used in this paper adheres to the concepts of dynamic similarity. This could
potentially make the development of realistic gaits easier. This paper has additionally shown
that coupled van der pol oscillators can be used in order to produce gaits that follow the
results of real animal data seen from alexanders work, and therefore are a realistic estimation
of an animals actual walking gaits.
One of the main findings of this paper, was that irregardless of parameters tested, the ma-
jority of walking gaits performed tended within the amounts seen in the research of Alexander
& Jayes (1983). Values above this tended to either experience unrealistic oscillator time-steps,
which may not be applicable in reality, or broke down and did not work properly.
Additional work should be done on effects of parameters, especially the effects that
changes in van der pol oscillator parameters have on the system, this paper shows a valid
initial breakdown of the effects of changing different parameter values on a simulated robot,
with a valid comparison to real life froude number. This work additionally provides a basis
for the measurement and creation of animal gaits, building a solid foundation from which
other investigations can occur.
